There are quite a lot of people who listen to artists like Fleetwood Mac, Pink Floyd, or the Beatles to show off how “alternative” and “counterculture” they are. They often position themselves as “thinker who are bored of contemporary stuff”, showing off their “pop culture fatigue” in every single post they post on social media, regardless of the fact that these music were considered to be very mainstream at that time.

I cannot deny the fact that those music produced earlier had their own glamour, but we are at the pinnacle of music industry before AI-generated crappy music really began taking over the market: the advancements of technology has truly democratised music production, making songwriter accessible to everyone. We have the largest, ever-growing catalogue of alternative and experimental music than ever before, it’s just they weren’t on music charts.

This makes me wonder that in 30 years, or maybe even sooner, teenagers at that time would listen to Taylor Swift and praise how “authentic, poetic, and vintage” her music are. They would listen to The Tortured Poets Department as if they found a piece of gemstone from a landfill, our watching her music videos and exclaiming “how’s that possible?” Maybe we will see The Eras Tour merch being sold at a vintage shop for several hundred pounds, and that sounds hilarious. However, if people in the future are really exposed to over-saturated AI-generated music, then they listening to our coming-of-age discography would be something that hits different.
